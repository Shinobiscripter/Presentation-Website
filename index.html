Inertia

An object at motion stays in motion, and an object at rest stays at rest, unless acted upon by an external unbalanced force.

What Is Inertia?

Inertia is Newton’s First Law of Motion. It explains that objects resist changes in their motion unless an external force acts on them.

Types of Inertia

• Inertia of Rest – A book on a table won’t move unless pushed.
• Inertia of Motion – A rolling ball keeps moving unless slowed.
• Inertia of Direction – A car turning makes you lean sideways.

Force, Motion, and Energy

Force is a push or pull that can change an object’s motion. Motion is the change in position of an object caused by force.

Newton’s Laws of Motion

1st Law: Objects stay in motion or rest unless acted on by a force.
2nd Law: Force = mass × acceleration (F = m × a).
3rd Law: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.
